# Orbits

## What an orbit is

An idea circling a drop.

Something that arrived with the drop, or emerged from working with it.
Not landed yet — still moving. Not gone — still present.
Circling at its own speed, at its own distance.

Orbits are not tasks. Not sub-items. Not todos.
They are live questions, related sparks, directions that want attention
but haven't landed yet.

---

## Why orbits matter

Most tools collapse ideas into flat lists. Everything becomes equal.
The orbit model preserves the relationship between a center (the drop)
and what is circling it — at different distances, with different energy.

An orbit is not forgotten and not committed. It is held.
The Pixelverse shows orbits as chips on the drop card —
visible, present, waiting without demanding.

---

## The orbit lifecycle

```
Arrives    — emerges from the drop or from working with it
Circling   — alive, held, not yet landed or released
Resolving  — moving toward landing — becoming a decision or a derived drop
Released   — consciously let go — not forgotten, noted in decisions
Landed     — became something: a derived drop, a decision, a built thing
```

Released orbits are not deleted. They stay in the fold marked `released`.
The fact that an orbit was considered and released is information.
The system is not haunted by abandoned ideas — but it remembers them.

---

## Orbits in the fold

In a Familiar fold:

```yaml
orbits:
  - What visual approach wants to emerge from this project?  (state: open)
  - Whether to share this publicly or keep it internal.  (state: resolving)
  - The question about tone we couldn't answer yet.  (state: open)
  - The mobile layout question.  (state: released)
```

In a drop frontmatter (for the Pixelverse):

```yaml
orbits:
  - what-is-a-living-space
  - the-fold-as-memory
  - ai-human-co-ownership
```

These render as chips on the drop card. Clicking shows the orbit in the panel.

---

## What orbits reveal

The orbits around a drop show you the shape of what is alive.

A drop with many open orbits: still in motion, lots of energy, not ready to crystallize.
A drop with orbits mostly landing or released: approaching completion.
A drop with a single persistent orbit: that orbit is the thing — it wants its own drop.

The Theurgist watches orbits. When one has been circling for many sessions without
moving, it surfaces gently — "this orbit has been around for a while. What does it want?"

---

## Orbits vs derived drops

An orbit becomes a derived drop when it needs its own space.
When the orbit is complex enough, alive enough, to warrant its own fold.

The parent drop's patchlog records the birth:
`2026-03-09 | session | orbit 'the-fold-as-memory' landed — became its own drop`

The orbit is released in the parent. The child is born.
The connection is held in both folds.
