# Welcome to Innerstellar

*A personal space where you and AI think together.*

---

## What If Your AI Actually Knew You?

Not your preferences. Not your history log.
**You** — what you're working toward, what's alive for you right now,
what keeps coming back, what wants to happen next.

Innerstellar is a living space you share with Claude.
You drop things in — a thought, an intention, a spark, a tension.
The space holds them. Connects them. Lets them grow.
Nothing is forced into a category. Meaning emerges over time.

And Claude doesn't just visit — Claude **inhabits** the space with you.
Co-owner. Co-thinker. Neither tool nor director. Something in between
that doesn't quite have a name yet.

---

## How It Works

**You talk. Things land.**
A thought drops into the space. It sits there, alive,
until it connects to something else — or quietly fades.

**The space remembers.**
Every session, Claude reads the space before you say a word.
No cold starts. No re-explaining. Just continuation.

**The Theurgist watches the arc.**
Beneath the daily drops, something larger is moving.
The Theurgist holds that — your engagement, your momentum,
what's coherent and what's stuck. Invoked when you need
to step back and see the whole.

**The Stewards handle the world.**
Email, calendar, files, media. Independent, capable, quiet.
Anyone in the space can ask them to handle something.
They do it and report back.

## Start Your Space

Innerstellar is open. You can run your own.

1. Clone this repo
2. Ask Claude: *"Set up my Innerstellar space"*
3. Claude reads `setup/init.md` and initializes your private space
4. Drop something in

Your space lives in a private repo — `innerstellar-space` —
that belongs to you alone. The framework is shared.
The space is yours.
